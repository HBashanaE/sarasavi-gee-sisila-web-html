Department of Building Economics

Situated within the esteemed University of Moratuwa which is globally recognized for its unparalleled academic prowess, the Department of Building Economics has consistently demonstrated a dedication to excellence. Its rich history, dating back to its inception showcases the department's pivotal role in shaping the academic landscape of Sri Lanka.

The Department of Building Economics, a beacon of academic excellence and innovation that was established in 1983 by the late Professor Siri Caldera, has grown to become a cornerstone of the University's commitment to shaping future leaders in construction, economics, and management while being the pioneer and sole provider of two government undergraduate degrees B.Sc Quantity Surveying and B.Sc Facilities Management.

One of the testaments to our commitment to excellence is the accreditation of the Honours Degree of Bachelor of Science in Quantity Surveying. This landmark achievement of being acknowledged by prestigious bodies such as IQSSL (Institute of Quantity Surveyors Sri Lanka), RICS (Royal Institute of Chartered Surveyors), CICES (Chartered Institution of Civil Engineering Surveyors) and PAQS (Pacific Association of Quantity Surveyors) reflects the high standards and global recognition our program has garnered.

Our alumni who are dispersed worldwide occupy esteemed positions in renowned firms, a testament to the quality of education imparted by the Department of Building Economics. The department offers a range of postgraduate Masters degrees such as Construction Law and Dispute Resolution, Project Management and Occupational Safety & Health Management attracting research students who are eager to contribute to the advancement of their respective fields.

Under the visionary leadership of the present Head of Department Dr. Mrs. Anuradha Waidyasekara, the department has not only thrived but achieved remarkable milestones. Mrs. Anuradha's brilliant leadership, marked by openness, unwavering kindness and nurturing nature has guided us through numerous unprecedented achievements.

Beyond academics, the department recognizes the importance of a holistic education. Initiatives such as Sithuwili, Sarasavi Gee Sisila, the classical music extravaganza underscore our commitment to balancing the mental health of our students and enhancing their interactive skills. These endeavors along with other non-academic activities, contribute to a well-rounded education that goes beyond textbooks.

Unarguably, both the degrees B.Sc Quantity Surveying and B.Sc Facilities Management are tremendous marks of globally recognized quality, which distinguish its graduates and set them apart from the competitive professional landscape. Join us on this journey of academic excellence, innovation and global recognition at the Department of Building Economics, University of Moratuwa.
